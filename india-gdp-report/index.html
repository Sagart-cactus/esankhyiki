Share-Worthy Facts

📈
₹33 Lakh Crore GDP
India's nominal GDP crossed ₹33 lakh crore in 2024-25 — nearly 4 times what it was in 2011-12. The economy has grown at ~10% nominally every year on average.
#IndiaGDP #MoSPI #NationalAccounts
💪
Post-COVID Surge: 9.7%
After contracting 5.8% in 2020-21, India bounced back with 9.7% real growth in 2021-22 — the fastest recovery among major economies.
#IndiaRecovery #EconomicGrowth
🏭
Services Dominate at 54%
Financial services, trade, transport, and public administration together contribute over 54% of India's GVA. Manufacturing remains stuck at ~14%.
#ServiceEconomy #IndiaGVA
💰
Investment Rate: 30% of GDP
Gross Fixed Capital Formation has risen to nearly 30% of GDP in 2024-25, driven by a massive government infrastructure push and private capex recovery.
#CapitalFormation #InvestInIndia
🍏
Agriculture: 18% of GVA
Despite employing ~45% of the workforce, agriculture's share of GVA is just 18%. The sector has shown remarkable COVID resilience with positive growth even in 2020-21.
#AgricultureIndia #FarmEconomy
💲
Trade Deficit Persists
India's imports consistently exceed exports. In 2024-25, the goods & services trade deficit stood at ~₹7.7L Cr, reflecting energy import dependence and strong domestic demand.
#TradeDeficit #IndiaExports #IndiaImports

GDP Trend: Current vs Constant Prices

India's GDP at current prices has grown from ₹87.4 lakh crore (2011-12) to ₹330.7 lakh crore (2024-25 PE). At constant 2011-12 prices, real GDP grew from ₹87.4 to ₹188 lakh crore — a 2.15x increase reflecting actual economic expansion.

GDP at Current & Constant Prices (₹ Lakh Crore)

Key Insight: The gap between current and constant price GDP widens over time, reflecting cumulative inflation. Nominal GDP grew 3.8x while real GDP grew 2.15x over the period.
02

GDP Growth Rate

India's real GDP growth rate has averaged around 6.6% per year (excluding the COVID dip), making it one of the fastest-growing major economies in the world.

Real GDP Growth Rate (% YoY at Constant Prices)

Key Insight: The 2020-21 contraction of -5.8% was India's worst economic performance since independence. The V-shaped recovery saw 9.7% growth in 2021-22, followed by 7.6% in 2022-23.

Consumption Trends

Private Final Consumption Expenditure (PFCE) is the single largest component of India's GDP, reflecting the spending power of 1.4 billion consumers.

Private & Government Consumption (₹ Lakh Crore, Constant Prices)

Key Insight: Private consumption at constant prices grew from ₹49.1L Cr (2011-12) to ₹106.2L Cr (2024-25 PE) — a 2.16x increase. Government consumption surged during COVID from ₹18.8L Cr to ₹23.6L Cr.
07

Investment: Gross Fixed Capital Formation

GFCF measures investment in physical assets — factories, machinery, buildings, and infrastructure. It's the key driver of long-term economic growth.

GFCF Trend (₹ Lakh Crore, Current Prices)

Key Insight: GFCF nearly doubled from ₹34.3L Cr in 2014-15 to ₹98.9L Cr in 2024-25 PE. The investment rate (GFCF/GDP) has climbed from ~28% to ~30%, driven by government infrastructure spending.

Exports vs Imports

India's trade in goods and services — showing the persistent trade deficit driven largely by energy imports.

Exports & Imports of Goods & Services (₹ Lakh Crore, Current Prices)

Key Insight: India's trade deficit (imports minus exports) widened from ₹6.7L Cr in 2012-13 to ₹7.7L Cr in 2024-25. Exports grew 2.9x while imports grew 2.5x over the period, but imports still exceed exports consistently.
