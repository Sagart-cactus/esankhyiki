01

General IIP Trend

The Index of Industrial Production (IIP) measures changes in the volume of production across mining, manufacturing, and electricity sectors. India's General IIP has grown from the 2011-12 base of 100 to 152.6, with a sharp pandemic dip and strong recovery.

General IIP Index & Growth Rate (2012-13 to 2024-25)

Key insight: The pandemic year 2020-21 saw a −8.4% contraction, but the recovery in 2021-22 was equally dramatic at +11.4%. Since then, growth has stabilised at 4–6% annually.
02

Sectoral Indices

IIP is composed of three broad sectors: Mining, Manufacturing, and Electricity. While Manufacturing dominates with 77.6% weight, Electricity has been the standout performer — more than doubling since the base year.

Mining, Manufacturing & Electricity Indices (2012-13 to 2024-25)

Key insight: Electricity (208.6) has massively outpaced Manufacturing (150.6) and Mining (132.8). India's power generation capacity additions have driven this divergence.
03

Use-Based Classification

IIP is also classified by end-use: Primary goods, Capital goods, Intermediate goods, Infrastructure/Construction goods, Consumer Durables, and Consumer Non-durables. Each tells a different story about India's industrial demand structure.

Use-Based Indices (2012-13 to 2024-25)

Key insight: Infrastructure & Construction goods (188.2) lead all use-based categories, reflecting India's massive infra spending. Capital Goods (112.6) remain the weakest, barely above base despite recovery.
04

Capital vs Consumer Goods

Capital Goods reflect investment demand while Consumer goods signal household consumption. The contrast between volatile Capital Goods and steady Consumer Non-durables reveals the structural dynamics of Indian industry.

Capital Goods vs Consumer Durables vs Consumer Non-durables

Key insight: Consumer Non-durables (151.4) have been the most resilient — barely dipping even during COVID. Capital Goods (112.6) crashed 30% during the pandemic and is still recovering. Consumer Durables (128.0) sit in between.
05

Manufacturing Sub-sectors

Within Manufacturing, certain sub-sectors have dramatically outperformed others. Pharma and Basic Metals have more than doubled, while some traditional sectors have lagged behind.

Top Manufacturing Sub-sectors by Index Value — 2024-25

Key insight: Pharma (231.0) and Basic Metals (228.4) have more than doubled since the base year, reflecting India's growing pharmaceutical exports and steel production capacity.
06

Growth Rate Comparison

Which manufacturing sub-sectors grew the fastest in 2024-25? Electrical Equipment leads with a remarkable 22.4% growth, followed by Furniture at 16.8%. These growth leaders indicate structural shifts in India's manufacturing landscape.

Top Growth Rates by Sub-sector — 2024-25 (%)

Key insight: Electrical Equipment's 22.4% growth reflects surging demand for transformers, switchgear, and EV components. Furniture (16.8%) and Other Transport Equipment (11.5%) round out the top three.
