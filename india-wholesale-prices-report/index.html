01

WPI All Commodities Trend

The Wholesale Price Index (WPI) measures the average change in prices of goods at the wholesale level. With base year 2011–12=100, the All Commodities index captures price movements across primary articles, fuel, and manufactured products.

WPI All Commodities — January Values (Base 2011–12=100)

Key insight: WPI rose 44% in 13 years. The sharpest jump was between 2021–22 when commodity prices surged globally, pushing the index from 126.5 to 143.8 — a single-year leap of 13.7%.
02

Major Group Indices

WPI tracks five key sub-indices: All Commodities, Primary Articles, Fuel & Power, Manufactured Products, and the derived Food Index. Each tells a distinct story about India’s wholesale price dynamics.

All Major Group Indices — January Values (2013–2025)

Key insight: Primary Articles (189.7) and Food Index (191.5) have risen nearly 90% from base, while Manufactured Products (143.4) saw the most moderate increase. Fuel & Power had the wildest ride — crashing to 79.8 in 2016 before surging to 155.6 in 2023.
03

WPI Composition

WPI assigns different weights to each major group based on their share in wholesale trade. Manufactured Products dominate at 64%, while Primary Articles and Fuel & Power make up the rest. Comparing weights to actual index values reveals which groups are punching above their weight.

04

Food Price Story

The Food Index is a derived composite cutting across Primary Articles and Manufactured Products. It tracks wholesale prices of food items — the most politically sensitive component of inflation in India.

Food Index & YoY Growth (%) — January Values

Key insight: Food inflation has been persistently high, averaging ~4.5% annually. The 2020 spike (+10%) and 2025 surge (+7.5%) show food prices remain India’s inflation pain point, driven by erratic monsoons and supply bottlenecks.
05

Fuel & Power Volatility

Fuel & Power is the most volatile component of WPI, swinging dramatically with global crude oil prices. This group includes coal, mineral oils, and electricity — each responding to different supply-demand dynamics.

Fuel & Power Index — January Values (2013–2025)

Key insight: Fuel prices crashed from 119.9 in 2014 to 79.8 in 2016 (−33%), then surged to 155.6 in 2023 (+95%). This rollercoaster reflects global crude oil price swings. The recent stabilization around 152–155 suggests a new normal.
06

Primary vs Manufactured

Comparing Primary Articles (raw materials) with Manufactured Products (finished goods) reveals the input-output price dynamics of Indian manufacturing. A widening gap signals margin pressure for producers.

Primary Articles vs Manufactured Products — Index Comparison

Key insight: The gap between Primary (189.7) and Manufactured (143.4) indices has widened from 8 percentage points in 2013 to 46pp in 2025. Raw material prices are rising much faster than finished goods, squeezing manufacturing margins.
07

YoY Inflation Rates

Year-on-year WPI inflation measures the percentage change in the index from one January to the next. Positive values indicate rising prices; negative values indicate deflation at the wholesale level.

WPI All Commodities — YoY Inflation Rate (%)

Key insight: WPI saw deflation in 2015–16 when global commodity prices crashed. The 2022 spike of 13.7% was the highest in a decade, driven by post-COVID supply disruptions and surging energy costs. The rate has since moderated to 2.5%.
