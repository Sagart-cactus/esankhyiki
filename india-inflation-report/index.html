01

CPI General Trend

The Consumer Price Index (CPI) measures changes in the price level of a weighted basket of consumer goods and services. India's CPI has risen steadily from the 2012 base, with Rural CPI consistently above Urban.

CPI General Index — Rural, Urban & Combined (Jan, 2014–2025)

Key insight: Rural CPI (195.9) has been consistently higher than Urban (190.6) across the entire period, reflecting higher food weights in rural consumption baskets.
02

Headline Inflation Rate

Year-on-year inflation rate for the General CPI (Combined). The RBI targets 4% inflation with a ±2% tolerance band (2–6%). In January 2020, headline inflation spiked to 7.59% driven by food prices.

YoY Inflation Rate — CPI General Combined (%)

Key insight: After the 2020 food-driven spike (7.59%), inflation stayed elevated through 2022–23 (above 6%) before moderating to 4.26% in Jan 2025 — back within the RBI comfort zone.
03

Food Inflation Deep Dive

Food & Beverages carries the highest weight (~46%) in India's CPI basket. Within food, Vegetables, Cereals, and Oils & Fats are the most volatile drivers. Food prices can swing wildly year-to-year due to monsoons, supply disruptions, and global commodity prices.

Food & Beverages CPI vs Consumer Food Price Index (Jan, 2014–2025)

Food Subgroup Indices — January 2025

Key insight: Spices (225.8), Meat & Fish (224.4), and Vegetables (217.8) have risen the most since 2012. Oils & Fats (184.1) are recovering after a sharp deflation phase. Sugar (130.5) remains well-controlled.

05

Fuel & Light

Fuel & Light CPI tracks prices of LPG, kerosene, firewood, electricity, and other cooking/lighting fuels. It saw a massive surge in 2022–23 due to the Russia-Ukraine conflict, followed by deflation as prices corrected.

Fuel & Light CPI Index & Inflation Rate (Jan, 2014–2025)

Key insight: Fuel & Light inflation peaked at 10.84% in Jan 2023, then turned negative in 2024 (−0.6%) and 2025 (−1.49%) — one of the few deflationary components in the basket.
06

Housing

Housing CPI measures rents and housing-related costs. It's only measured in the Urban sector and then weighted into Combined. Housing inflation spiked to 11.27% in 2014, hit 8.33% in 2018, and has since moderated to around 3%.

Housing CPI Index & Inflation Rate (Jan, 2014–2025)

Key insight: After peaking at 11.27% inflation in Jan 2014, Housing CPI has gradually moderated. The index value of 182.6 still means rents are 83% higher than in 2012.
07

Rural vs Urban Inflation

Rural and Urban India experience inflation differently. Rural baskets have higher food weights (~54% vs ~36%), making rural inflation more sensitive to food price shocks. Urban baskets have higher services and housing weights.

Rural vs Urban CPI Inflation Rate (Jan, 2014–2025)

Key insight: Rural inflation was lower during the 2019 food deflation period (1.22% vs 2.91%) but surged higher during food spikes like 2020 (7.73% vs 7.39%). The gap reflects different consumption patterns.

08

State-wise CPI

CPI levels vary significantly across Indian states, reflecting different consumption patterns, local supply conditions, and market structures. Manipur records the highest CPI (220.3) while Delhi has the lowest (171.9) in January 2025.

State-wise CPI General Index — January 2025 (Top 20 States/UTs)

Key insight: Northeastern states (Manipur 220.3, Tripura 208.2) and southern states (Kerala 203.6, Telangana 202.3) have the highest CPI levels. Delhi (171.9) and Meghalaya (180.8) are at the lower end.
09

Services & Miscellaneous

The Miscellaneous group covers Health, Education, Transport & Communication, Housing Goods, Recreation, and Personal Care. These services-driven subgroups have been a source of persistent “core” inflation.

Miscellaneous Subgroup Indices (Jan, 2014–2025)

Key insight: Personal Care & Effects (209.2) and Health (198.8) have grown the fastest among services. Transport & Communication (171.4) remains relatively restrained despite fuel volatility.
