Total Primary Energy Supply

India's total primary energy supply has grown from ~594,000 KToE in 2012-13 to over 903,000 KToE in 2023-24 — a 52% increase in twelve years, powering the world's fastest-growing major economy.

Total Primary Energy Supply Trend (KToE)

Key Insight: COVID-19 caused the only dip in 2020-21 (710,062 KToE, a 6.8% drop from 2019-20), but recovery was swift with a 27% surge by 2023-24.

Domestic Production vs Imports

India produces about 58% of its energy domestically but relies heavily on imports for crude oil and increasingly for coal.

Production vs Imports (KToE)

Key Insight: Imports grew 55% (296,176 → 459,250 KToE) while production grew 48% (353,332 → 521,633 KToE). Imports now equal 88% of domestic production, up from 84%.
04

Import Dependency Deep Dive

How much of each major fuel does India import?

Coal: Domestic Production vs Imports

Coal imports nearly doubled from 74,275 KToE (2012-13) to 141,639 KToE (2023-24) — a 91% increase. Import share rose from 23% to 26%.

Crude Oil: Domestic Production vs Imports

India produces just 11% of its crude oil needs domestically (30,002 KToE). Crude oil imports hit 239,415 KToE, up 27% from 2012-13.

Clean Energy Sources (KToE)

Oil Products: The Diesel Nation

A breakdown of India's petroleum product consumption reveals Diesel's overwhelming dominance.

Oil Product Consumption 2023-24 (KToE)

Diesel accounts for 39% of all oil product consumption (93,064 KToE). Petrol/Motor Spirit is the second largest at 39,826 KToE. LPG consumption has grown 51% in 8 years, reflecting the Ujjwala scheme's success in expanding clean cooking fuel access.
10

The Disappearing Kerosene

One of the most dramatic shifts in India's energy story.

Kerosene vs LPG Consumption (KToE)

Kerosene collapsed 93% from 7,035 KToE (2014-15) to 501 KToE (2023-24), while LPG surged 65% from 20,332 to 33,506 KToE. This substitution reflects one of India's most successful clean energy transitions — millions of households moved from kerosene lamps to LPG stoves.
