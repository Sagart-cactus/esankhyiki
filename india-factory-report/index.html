01

Factory Growth Trend

The number of factories in operation under the Factories Act has grown steadily over 8 years, crossing the 2-lakh mark in 2020–21 and accelerating in the most recent years.

Factories in Operation — All India (2016–17 to 2023–24)

Key insight: Factory count grew 10% from 1.94L to 2.13L, with the sharpest jump in 2022–23 (+6,000) and 2023–24 (+6,500). The steady rise reflects ongoing industrial formalization.
02

Output vs Input

Total output and total input costs have both grown sharply, but the gap between them — the Gross Value Added (GVA) — has widened, indicating improved value addition by India’s factory sector.

Total Output vs Total Inputs — Rs Lakh Crore

Key insight: Output doubled from Rs 72.7L Cr to Rs 153.3L Cr. The Output-Input gap (GVA) widened from Rs 13.7L Cr to Rs 24.6L Cr, reflecting improved value addition in India’s industrial base.
03

Profitability Trend

Net profit of India’s factory sector has seen dramatic swings — a COVID-era dip followed by a sharp recovery. By 2023–24, profits had nearly doubled from the 2016–17 level.

Net Profit — Rs Lakh Crore

Key insight: Net profit nearly doubled from Rs 5.4L Cr to Rs 10.7L Cr. The COVID year 2019–20 saw a dip to Rs 4.7L Cr, followed by a sharp recovery to Rs 9.5L Cr by 2021–22.
04

Employment Trend

Factory employment encompasses persons engaged (total headcount), total workers (production + non-production), and direct workers. All three categories have grown, but the gap between total and direct workers has widened as contract hiring rises.

Employment — Persons Engaged, Total Workers, Direct Workers (Crores)

Key insight: Employment grew 31% from 1.49 Cr to 1.96 Cr persons engaged. Contract workers grew faster than direct workers, reflecting the contractualization trend in India’s organised manufacturing sector.
05

Gender in Factories

Female participation in India’s factory workforce remains low. While the absolute numbers have grown, women continue to constitute less than one-fifth of direct factory workers.

Male vs Female Direct Workers — Lakhs

Key insight: Female worker share remains stubbornly low at ~18% of direct workers. Female employment dipped during COVID (14.2L in 2020–21) but recovered to 16.4L by 2023–24.
06

Direct vs Contract Workers

The composition of India’s factory workforce is shifting. Contract workers now make up over 40% of total workers, up from around 35% in 2016–17. This trend raises questions about job quality and worker protections.

Direct Workers vs Contract Workers — Lakhs

Key insight: Contract workers grew from ~40L to 64.5L, outpacing direct workers who grew from 74L to 90.7L. The contractualization of India’s factory workforce continues, with contract workers now at 41.6% of total workers.
07

Cost & Employment Structure

Understanding the cost composition and workforce mix of India’s factory sector in 2023–24 reveals structural patterns — input costs dominate output, while the workforce is split between direct and contract labour.

Cost Structure — 2023–24 (% of Output)

Employment Mix — 2023–24

Key insight: Input costs dominate at ~84% of output. Fuel consumption fell slightly in 2023–24 (Rs 6.03L Cr vs Rs 6.10L Cr in 2022–23), suggesting energy efficiency gains. Contract workers now constitute 41.6% of total workers.
